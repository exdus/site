Caffeine Effects on your body

Caffeine And It's Effect On Your Brain

 

Coffee, tea, and soda are the three greatest source of caffeine in our diets. Within 30 minutes of consumption, caffeine reaches its peak level in the blood.

Caffeine can have a negative effect on our bones. The more regularly a woman takes coffee, the more calcium is excreted in her urine. Upto 65mg of calcium for every six ounces of coffee or two cans of soda, thus a contributing factor osteoporosis.

It also has detrimental effects on the brain. It undermines the functioning of the frontal lobe by tampering with important chemicals: acetylcholine, adenosine, and dopamine.

By increasing the the levels of acetylcholine and dopamine thus interfering with the transmission of adenosine. Adenosine slows down many functions of the brain nerve transmission.

This function is weakend by caffeine thus allowing artificial stimulation that is known to occur with caffeinated drinks. Some psychiatric disorders such as schizophrenia are in part due to elavated levels of dopamine. There also seem to be a link to decreased frontal lobe functioning and blood flow that is characteristic of depression.

In order to compensate for the changes that occur with chronic caffeine consumption the brain actually alters its chemical structure. In so doing, the brain chemistry becomes unbalanced and is therefore dependent or addicted to the regular consumption of caffeine.

Withdrawals symptons which may include headaches, fatigue, sleepiness, laziness, decreased alertness, and activity, often begin within 19 hours after the last consumption.

Some effects of caffeine include impaired physical and mental perfomance, interrupted sleep, and impacted spiritual and social dimensions of our character.It can cause toxicity and even death; it may be a carcinogen; it increases risk of low birth weight in infants,elavates cholesterol, it can cause palpitations and other dangerous heart rhythms , it stimulates excess stomach acid and heartburn, and it may increase symptoms of PMS (Premenstrual Syndrome).

"When these tea and coffee users meet together for social entertainment; the effects of their pernicious habit are manifest. All partake freely of their favourite beverages, and as the stimulating influence is felt, their tongues are loosened, and they begin the wicked work of talking against others.
Their words are not few or well chosen. The tidbits of gossip are passed around, too often the poison of scandal as well. These thoughtless gossipers forget that they have a witness. An unseen Watcher is writing their words in the books of heaven."

There you have it, something to ponder about before you refill your favourite coffee mug.

Christian Temperance and Bible Hygiene, 36.
